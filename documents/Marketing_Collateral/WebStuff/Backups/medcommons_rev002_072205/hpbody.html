MedCommons is a service for secure digital transfer of patient health information records (PHI) among providers, patients, and their consultants under explicit patient consent. Patients 'opt in' to PHI transfers by signing consent forms with their providers. This also allows MedCommons to support the provider’s HIPAA responsibilities.

For interoperability and archival longevity, MedCommons uses the ASTM Continuity of Care Record (CCR) standard for transfer of PHI. Diagnostic imaging and legacy documents such as physicians’ reports are also handled in a non-proprietary manner.

MedCommons delivers increased productivity for physicians whether or not they have installed an electronic medical record system. MedCommons can be used for faster electronic referrals, secure patient communications that are more efficient than the phone, and as a highly integrated yet individualized presentation of the services they access or provide.

All of this is provided by MedCommons in a manner that is automatically accessible to the patient and automatically incorporated into their personal health record repository that only they control.

 
MedCommons is the patient's bank for personal health information.
Physicians save time when they use a standard designed and controlled by their peers.
Imaging facilities and diagnostic labs achieve unprecedented integration of their services.
Specialists extend their referral base across traditional enterprise and technical barriers.
ASTM CCR is the physician-inspired interoperability standard.
