Do You Ever Forget Your Pills? New Web-Service from CARETEXT Reminds You On Your Cell Phone!

PR Newswire via NewsEdge Corporation : PHILADELPHIA, May 11 /PRNewswire/ -- If you ever forget to take your medication, or want to make sure you never forget, log on right now to http://www.caretext.com.

Whether you are a young woman on oral contraceptives, an executive juggling a job and hypertension, a diabetic who forgets to check your blood sugar or a woman on post-menopausal medications, CARETEXT is a personal medication reminder service that harnesses the power of your cellular phone to create a reliable, private way to remember your medication needs.

CARETEXT allows you to securely create and send personalized, scheduled text messages that are sent directly to your telephone. You decide what to say and you set the time and date of each reminder.

Whether you have a daily, weekly, or a very complex multiple medication schedule ... whether for prevention or for chronic conditions such as hypertension, heart disease, diabetes or asthma ... or even if just for a short-term course of antibiotics ... CARETEXT can help.

Studies have shown that missed medications are a major reason for poor medical outcomes, especially for preventive medications and for chronic conditions.

Consistently missing doses of medicine can have a devastating effect on people down the road, noted Steven Merahn, MD, the pediatrician and public health specialist who founded CARETEXT. Lots of reminder programs have been tried, but nothing seems to have the same power and intimacy as the cell phone.

Anyone with a cell phone can use the service. Registration is simple and secure; your personal information is completely confidential.

Simply log on to CARETEXT and the service will ask how many meds you are on and create a unique schedule, no matter how complicated. Users can either choose prewritten messages or write their own to remember doses, prescription renewals or any other health needs.

CARETEXT is currently available at no cost to all users. CARETEXT is designed to be part of patient web portals for hospital systems, health plans and managed care (formulary-specific versions of the service are available). CARETEXT also offers disease-category exclusivity for single-branded sites of pharmaceutical companies.

We are also offering some health education messages and humor along with the reminders, added Dr. Merahn. We think CARETEXT can really add value to any disease management program.

For more information about CARETEXT, or to discuss specialized CARETEXT services for health plans, managed care, disease management programs or branded pharmaceuticals, contact Steven Merahn, MD, by phone at 215-832-0181 or via email mailto:smerahn@cliniscience.com .

1. NEW MOBILE-PHONE REMINDER SERVICE TARGETING PHARMA PARTNERSHIPS
The service, called Caretext, is designed to send personalized, scheduled text messages directly to a cell phone, with the goal of improving patients' medication adherence and persistence. The medication-reminder tool is being offered for free to consumers, who can log onto a website to program their text messages and mobile-phone number, the company says. Steven Merahn, MD, founder of Caretext, told ePharmaceuticals via e-mail that the firm is discussing "category-exclusive licenses" with three pharmcos, two to provide a value-added service for the brand website and the other to offer as a sponsored service to hospital systems as part of disease management. Merahn says he envisions partnering with hospital systems, health plans, and MCOs to make Caretext part of their patient portals.

2. TUFTS STARTS EVIDENCE-BASED TOOL TO HELP MEMBERS HOSPITAL SHOP
Tufts Health Plan says its members now can use a set of online decision-support tools from HealthShare Technology to help them shop for a hospital. Tufts was among a number of New England's largest insurers which The Boston Globe (ePharm5 3/11/04) said were planning to let patients go online to search for top-rated hospitals. Tufts added the tool to its technology offering, Tufts Health eAccess. The Web-based comparison tool is designed to give members an objective, condition-specific comparison of hospital treatment outcomes for 165 diagnoses or procedures, and to provide personalized access to healthcare performance data. Other carriers expected to offer online hospital comparison tools, according to the Globe, include Blue Cross Blue Shield of Massachusetts and Anthem Blue Cross and Blue Shield.

3. STOREFRONT OFFERS TO FILL BACK ORDERS AMONG CAN. WEB PHARMACIES
As supply problems lead to order backlogs among some Canadian pharmacies, a nation-wide network of local storefronts in the United States is offering to fill those prescriptions from Great Britain. The American Drug Club says it will fill the backordered prescriptions by sourcing drugs from an undisclosed London pharmacy. The club has franchises in 12 states. Through offices or kiosks, it lets customers find information about obtaining Canadian prescription drugs from a network of pharmacies. It's not the only one offering the U.K. option. Many Canadian online pharmacies (ePharm5 04/15/04), and two states (ePharm5 5/12/04), have looked to foreign countries for Rx drugs following pharmco efforts intended to stifle the cross-border import trade.

4. PFIZER: R&D EFFORTS COULD SUFFER IF PRICING PRESSURE MOUNTS
Pfizer wants to get the word out about its R&D program in the hope that promoting its research efforts and their positive impact will stem consumer demand for lower drug prices, reports The St. Louis Post-Dispatch. Pfizer CEO Hank McKinnell said the company wants to make consumers understand why some drugs are so expensive and therefore make them feel the drugs are worth buying in the United States. "The fundamental problem we have is...people don't value what we do," McKinnell said. Political pressure to force prices down, which continues to build, could put drug development in some jeopardy, concurred a director of economic analysis for the Tufts Center for the Study of Drug Development. One major fear of price-cutting is that erosion in ROI could lead to cuts in Pfizer jobs and in its $8-billion annual R&D budget.

5. IAB STUDY: ONLINE ADS OUTPERFORM TV IN AUTOMAKER'S CAMPAIGN
The Interactive Advertising Bureau says online ads outperformed television for dollar-for-dollar cost efficiency in a recent Ford Motor Co. campaign, ClickZ reports. The findings were released as part of IAB's latest Cross Media Optimization Study. Although TV garnered the greatest level of absolute reach and purchase consideration impact, online ads outperformed TV in cost efficiency, the study showed. For every dollar spent online, $21 was allocated for TV. Ford's campaign included online ad placements such as roadblocks -- ads that interrupt homepages -- and in-market ads, which are directed toward people in the market for a car. Search generated the strongest correlation with sales. People who did searches on truck-related keywords were more than four times as likely to buy than people who didn't search those terms, according to IAB.
