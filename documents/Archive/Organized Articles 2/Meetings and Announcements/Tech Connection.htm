1. PRESIDENTIAL SEARCH COMMITTEES FORMING
As we emailed last week, Chuck Vest announced his intention to step
down from the presidency of MIT by the beginning of the 2004 fall 
term or whenever his successor is appointed. Jim Champy '63 has been 
asked to chair the Corporation Committee on the President, and the 
faculty officers, led by Chair of the Faculty Rafael Bras '72, have 
been asked to create a faculty advisory committee. These committees 
should be formed and announced by mid-January. Students, staff, and 
alumni will also have an opportunity to provide input to the search 
process, and the Association will keep alumni informed as the process 
develops. To view the press announcement and a letter from Chuck to 
the MIT faculty and staff, see the MIT News Office Web site at: 
http://web.mit.edu/newsoffice/nr/2003/cmv.html

2. ASSOCIATION HAPPENINGS
As a cost-saving measure, MIT will have a one-time holiday closing 
from Dec. 25, 2003 through Jan. 4, 2004. This will include the 
Alumni Association's offices. Though the campus will be closed, some 
essential services will still be available. We will have staff on 
hand to receive and process year-end gifts. For any gift or pledge 
questions during the holiday closing please call 617-253-8270. For 
more information about the holiday closing and Institute budget 
issues, go to: http://web.mit.edu/finances 
 
On January 21 at 7:00pm, the MIT Enterprise Forum presents the 
satellite broadcast, "Innovation at the Interface: Technological 
Fusion at MIT," a program moderated by MIT Sloan School Professor 
Ed Roberts and featuring Dr. Robert Langer, Professor of Chemical 
and Biomedical Engineering, and Dr. Rodney Brooks, Director of the 
MIT Computer Science and Artificial Intelligence Laboratory. The 
topic is how technologies are converging to create unprecedented 
business opportunity at the cutting edge. To attend in person at 
Kresge Auditorium, or to see if there is a satellite viewing site 
near you, please visit http://web.mit.edu/entforum/SBSnext.html 
or for more information, contact mitef@mit.edu or 617-253-0015.

4. PARTICIPATE. DESIGNATE. MAKE A DIFFERENCE TODAY
The annual gifts from alumni have an enormous impact on MIT. The 
Institute's rise to world leadership in education and research has 
been due, in part, to generations of generosity. Annual giving will 
help sustain that position of leadership in the years to come. You 
can designate your gift to a program you support, or make a gift of 
unrestricted funds allowing MIT to apply the funds where they are 
needed most. Make an immediate impact by making your tax-deductible 
gift to MIT today! https://alum.mit.edu/giftform/GiftMain.dyn
 
Gifts made through December 26 via our secure online gift form can 
still be processed for the 2003 tax year. And don't forget to take 
advantage of your company's matching gift program! You can learn 
about your company's matching gift policies at: 
http://www.matchinggifts.com/mit/

8. MIT RESEARCH UPDATE:
 
Michael Hawley of the MIT Media Lab recently landed himself in the 
"Guinness Book of World Records" by creating the largest book ever 
published. "Bhutan: A Visual Odyssey Across the Himalayan Kingdom" 
contains more than 100 digital photographs of the region, weighs 
in at more than 130 pounds, and stands at five feet by seven feet. 
http://web.mit..edu/newsoffice/nr/2003/hawley.html 
 
Individuals with a common genetic variation in one of two known 
estrogen receptors have a threefold increase in the risk of having 
a heart attack. http://web.mit.edu/newsoffice/nr/2003/heart.html
 
The Education Arcade, a new initiative involving MIT researchers, 
aims to transform the way video and computer games are used in the 
classroom. http://web.mit.edu/newsoffice/nr/2003/educade.html 
 
Large molecules composed of subunits designed to perform specific 
tasks can detect pollutants in water, help jets maneuver at high 
speeds and improve the efficiency of internal combustion engines 
http://web.mit..edu/newsoffice/nr/2003/nocera.html 
 
In a finding that may help create unlimited quantities of 
therapeutically valuable adult stem cells, an MIT researcher 
fortified adult rat liver stem cells with a metabolite that allows 
them to multiply like embryonic stem cells. 
http://web.mit.edu/newsoffice/nr/2003/sherley.html
