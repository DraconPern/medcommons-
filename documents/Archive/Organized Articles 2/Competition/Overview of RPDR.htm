Description

The Research Patient Data Registry (RPDR) serves as a central clinical data registry, or data warehouse. The RPDR gathers data from various hospital legacy systems and stores it in one place. Researchers access this data using the RPDR online query tool. They may query the RPDR data for aggregate totals and, with proper IRB approval, obtain medical record data. The RPDR brings clinical information to the researcher's fingertips and ensures the security of patient information by controlling and auditing the distribution of patient data within the guidelines of the IRB.

The use of this application compares and contrasts with prior patient cohort search methodologies such as medical record chart review and the use of Information System requests. All these methods ultimately return a set of patients that match certain criteria. They differ in that the RPDR allows the user to perform the search on-line in real time. This results in faster turnaround and greater control using the RPDR. The alternatives also leave the confidentiality of the Partners' patient much more exposed, often returning entire medical records when only focused information is required.

Functions of RPDR
The RPDR has two related but separate functions. First, an online query tool provides aggregate numbers of patients with user-defined characteristics such as diagnoses, procedures and/or medications. Patient identifiers are not available at this function level. Secondly, the RPDR data acquisition engine allows the user to receive more detailed medical record information about the queried patients.

 

The online query tool searches through approximately 50 million patient records in order to return the query results to the user, usually within seconds. Once the user has obtained the aggregate numbers of patients with the defined characteristics, the user can then request, with IRB approval, more detailed information on these patients. Users can also supply the RPDR with their own patient Medical Record Numbers (MRNs) in order to obtain medical record data, again with IRB approval. This medical record data can include demographic and encounter information, laboratory results, discharge summaries, operative notes, radiology and pathology reports, all returned in text reports and/or a Microsoft Access database.

Summary of RPDR features:
· Automated on-line registration for faculty members who have a Partners logon by going to the web address: http://rpdrweb/partners/rpdrinfo/start/
· Query about patients using diagnoses, inpatient procedures, demographics, inpatient medications, and provider type data.
· Query BWH and MGH inpatients and outpatients billed through the TSI and IDX billing systems.
· Find deceased patients flagged using data from the national Social Security database.
· Access all previous queries to allow building upon prior queries.
· On-line Help, Tutorial and Workgroup management.
· Identify patients (with proper IRB approval).
· Availability of wet laboratory results with requests for identified patient data.
· Availability of text files for patient discharge summaries, radiology reports, pathology reports and operative notes.

Data in RPDR
The RPDR data warehouse contains patient demographic data, diagnoses and procedure data, inpatient pharmacy data, and assorted data on inpatient hospitalization and clinical encounters including provider information.

· Population
The goal of the RPDR is to provide clinical information for as much of the inpatient and outpatient population at the MGH and BWH as possible. The database contains those inpatients and outpatients from BWH and MGH billed through the TSI billing system and inpatients and outpatients billed through the MGH MGPOs and through the seven BWH billing foundations. The RPDR population includes 90-98% of the total patient population at BWH and MGH from 1993 to present.
· Patient Demographic Data
The RPDR extracts patient demographic data from the Enterprise Master Patient Index (EMPI). The EMPI assigns a 9-digit reference number to a patient and serves as a mechanism to assign multiple medical record numbers to one reference number, and eliminates the problem of one patient being duplicated in the RPDR. The RPDR database imports and maintains the following demographic information that the user may query: Gender, Age, Language, Race, Marital Status, Religion, Veteran Status, State, Zip, Country, and vital status as determined by matching against a social security deceased file. RPDR also imports identifiable demographic data from the EMPI, which is available to RPDR users with the appropriate IRB approval. This data includes patient name, address, city, social security number, home phone number, work phone number, date of birth, and primary care provider.
· Diagnosis Data
Through the RPDR a user may query diagnoses data to obtain aggregate totals of various diagnoses and groups of diagnoses. The RPDR contains BWH inpatient and outpatient diagnosis information from 10/96, MGH inpatient data from 10/92 and MGH outpatient information from 10/93. RPDR identifies diagnosis by ICD-9-CM standards. A user has the ability to query diagnoses through the use of a hierarchical tree structure, thus eliminating the need for the user to know the exact ICD-9-CM terms used by the RPDR.
· Procedure Data
Inpatient procedure data is also available to RPDR users. Users may obtain aggregate information about inpatient and some hospital-based outpatient procedures performed at MGH and BWH. Inpatient procedure information is available from October 1992 for MGH patients and from October 1996 for BWH patients. Outpatient procedure information for the MGH and BWH is limited to "observation" patients, surgical day patients and ER patients. RPDR identifies procedures by ICD-9-CM standards.
· Medication Data
Inpatient medication data is available from October 1992 for MGH patients and from October 1996 for BWH patients. Outpatient medication information for the MGH and BWH is limited to "observation" patients, surgical day patients and ER patients. Medication data is obtained through cost codes generated by the TSI billing systems.
· Provider Information
The RPDR database stores provider information. Individual provider information is rolled up into specialty categories to permit users to query for aggregate information by institution and department. Users cannot query against an individual provider without IRB approval. The RPDR database stores the provider's name, specialty, ID number and the site where the provider practices.
· Encounter information
Encounter (or visit) information is maintained so patients can be chosen only from one hospital, or from specific clinics, or by inpatient/outpatient status.


Institutional Review Board
The RPDR is a clinical patient data registry. The Institutional Review Board (IRB) defines a clinical patient data registry as a database that contains a limited set of data that may be derived from an electronic medical record, laboratory database, and other databases. A registry does not contain the vast amount of information that is typically part of a complete patient medical record, paper or electronic. The purpose of the registry is to provide clinical investigators with the ability to perform a variety of queries for pre-study, pre-trial patient cohort identification. The queries provide aggregate information and are designed to deny access to information about individual patients or physicians. Under federal regulations, electronic searches of clinical patient data registries fall into a category of research that may be classified as exempt from IRB approval if the IRB so chooses. Therefore, in 1997 the Information Unit of the Clinical Research Program, led by Henry Chueh, MD, proposed the following to the Partners IRB, led by Greg Koski, MD:

· To designate such searches as exempt from review
· To approve classes of users for access to the registries

The result is that users are approved rather than individual queries. If the user wishes to get specific patient or provider information, the user must obtain IRB approval through the normal review process. In accordance with the IRB regulations noted above, the RPDR will return medical records to those users who have obtained the appropriate level of IRB approval. All queries will be audited by user name, time and date of query and query criteria. Audit logs will be available continuously for review by the IRB and/or Medical Records.


Medical Record Information
Medical record information will be returned to those users with the appropriate IRB approval. Users requesting medical record information through the RPDR will be required to enter an IRB-maintained password. The RPDR system will then verify that the IRB/password is correct. Additionally, the researcher requesting information is required to list a designated MGH sponsor to receive MGH data and a BWH sponsor to receive BWH information. This designated sponsor must be pre-approved by the IRB for the given IRB number. The RPDR system will verify this. Once the above information is verified, the RPDR will return the medical record information to a shared drive that is only accessible by the designated sponsored users. Therefore, a single request for both MGH and BWH data will result in the MGH data being returned to a shared drive only accessible by the MGH sponsor and the BWH data being returned to a shared drive only accessible by the BWH sponsor.


Registration
In accordance with the IRB regulations noted above, users will have to be defined within the RPDR application as authorized and to have a Partners NT logon ID. BWH and MGH appointed staff with a faculty appointment are eligible to become faculty sponsors and add and manage their own workgroup members. Eligibility of faculty sponsors is determined during RPDR registration. The faculty sponsor may then access the RPDR Query Tool from among the Partners' applications via the Start windows command. Faculty sponsors can utilize the query themselves and/or add workgroup members from their research teams. The Workgroup section, located within the RPDR Information page, is used by faculty sponsors for adding members, monitoring the use of the tool and approving identified data requests from workgroup members. The RPDR Information Page includes the Workgroup section; http://rpdrweb/partners/rpdrinfo/start/ . To register you must first have a Partners logon.

The use of the query tool by workgroup members to obtain aggregate numbers of defined patients has additional security measures. The workgroup members will receive approximate numbers of patients for their query results, utilizing a statistical algorithm within the application. The data obfuscation methodology provides an additional layer of protection to the confidentiality of Partners' patients. Workgroup members can request identified patient data by submitting the request through the faculty sponsor. All workgroup member requests for identified patient data must be reviewed and approved via electronic password by the faculty sponsor, as determined by electronic submission requirements within the application.

Technology
The RPDR data warehouse is a Microsoft SQL-Server 7.0 database and sits on an NT platform. This means that the RPDR database and servers are supported by the Partners IS server team. Data in the RPDR can be accessed from Partners workstations and from non-standard PC's and Macintoshes via a Citrix client. Users access the data warehouse through a web-based front-end query tool, requiring users to have MS Explorer 4.0 or greater. The RPDR query tool is not accessible via Netscape.
