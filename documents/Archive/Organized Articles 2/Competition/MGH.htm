In a First, Benemax Adds Coverage for Online Second Opinions to Health
Benefit Plans - Affiliation with Partners HealthCare Puts 4,000
Specialists
 
 
PR Newswire via NewsEdge Corporation : MEDFIELD, Mass., Jan. 8
/PRNewswire/ -- Benemax, a recognized innovator in consumer-directed
health plans, is the first employee benefit management firm to offer an
online second opinion service from Harvard-affiliated Partners
Telemedicine as part of its health plans for employers.
 
Benemax's third-party coverage of this service is a tremendous advance in
employer-funded health benefits, said Joseph C. Kvedar, M.D., director of
Partners Telemedicine. Second opinions regarding medical diagnosis and
treatments have long been essential to achieving quality health care.
Benemax's recognition of this fact though its coverage for this service is
a great step towards better medical care," added Kvedar, who is also
president- elect of the American Telemedicine Association.
 
Employees covered under the Benemax plan can arrange, through their
doctors, for a second opinion from more than 4,000 specialists in the
Partners HealthCare network, including the Harvard-affiliated teaching
hospitals Massachusetts General, Brigham and Women's, and Dana-Farber
Cancer Institute. There is no cost to employee or employer.
 
Typically, within five days of submitting a request, the local physician
receives a detailed consultation and treatment plan based on symptoms
presented. Partners Telemedicine has found that in 90 percent of cases,
specialists from the Partners system recommend a significant change in
treatment, often resulting in a better outcome.
 
Employers can provide this exceptional service without increasing
premiums. In fact, we believe this program will improve health care
efficiency, thereby reducing employers' health benefit costs over time,
said David Cowles, executive vice president of Benemax. Physician and
patient can consult with top-flight specialists regarding a complex
diagnosis-without traveling thousands of miles.
 
Patients and their families who are dealing with a life-altering illness
want to uncover every stone, said Kvedar. It's often overwhelming to find
the right specialist and navigate the system. A second opinion through
Partners Telemedicine removes these obstacles. Care remains personal and
local as the doctor takes advantage of the expertise of specialists who
treat many patients with the same condition.
 
About Partners Telemedicine
 
Boston-based Partners Telemedicine (http://www.telemedicine.partners.org)
is a health care consultation service from Partners HealthCare System,
Inc. available to physicians worldwide to support patient care. Expertise
from knowledgeable practitioners is accessed though specialty
consultations online.
 
About Benemax
