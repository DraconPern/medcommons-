The main differences between regular DICOM imaging and RT imaging seems to
be:
 
 
*       Modality devices are slightly different - the table that a patient
is on in an RT planning device is similar to the one used during
treatement - so 
 
*       There are maps generated of effected organs; sometimes images are
segmented so that organs can be targeted (or avoided).
*       Lots of stuff on registration of different types of images
(different modalities; same device over time (measured in weeks)
*       Workflow is different (different department).
 
 
The basic network protcols and data serialization formats are identical to
what we already have.
