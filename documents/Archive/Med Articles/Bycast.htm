Archive and image distribution provider Bycast has garnered new funding,
signed a new client, and appointed a new executive chairman to its board
of directors. 
 
The Vancouver-based vendor was selected by Mercy Hospital in Miami to
provide its Bycast StorageGrid product to power Mercy’s enterprise medical
image archive. The product will integrate image archives from a Canon
<http://www.auntminnie.com/default.asp?sec=vdp&sub=vendors&pag=vendordetai
l&vendorid=21490>  Medical Systems PACS in the radiology department and a
Philips
<http://www.auntminnie.com/default.asp?sec=vdp&sub=vendors&pag=vendordetai
l&vendorid=21599>  Medical Systems PACS in the cardiology department,
Bycast said.
 
In other news, Bycast said it closed a $10 million round of funding from
new and existing investors. The financing was lead by XJ Partners, and all
significant existing shareholders participated, the company said.
 
Lastly, the firm has appointed William Tatham as the executive chairman of
its board of directors. Tatham succeeds the current acting chairman, Dr.
Moe Kermani, who remains a director on the board as well as Bycast’s
president and CEO.
 
By AuntMinnie.com staff writers
March 2, 2004
