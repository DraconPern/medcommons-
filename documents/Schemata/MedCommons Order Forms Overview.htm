MedCommons  Order Forms Overview 4/11/04

 

Here’s the background image part of the MedCommons Order Form we are putting into play. It’s been made to fit a standard page, and the resolution was lowered a bit.  Don’t worry, it’s just a test. It’s a plain old PDF now.

 

Here’s the same form with some live fields added to it.  The fields were added by Adobe Designer. There are a few items of note:

 

 

  • LastName and Street are live, and available for user input.

 

  • FormName and Recipe are hidden, readonly fields, with values embedded in the form. Presumably they will tell the receiver of these fields (MedCommons Central) what to do with the form.

 

  • TrackingNumber is read-only, and cannot be set from inside Acrobat.

 

  • There are cheesy buttons over Print and Submit.  The Submit button sends a mail message, but will be wired to do an HTTP Post

 

 

You can put values in the fields and hit the button over Submit. I’ve got it rigged right now to generate an email message with just the form field values. Try it. It works in Acrobat Profession 6.02. I have no idea whether it will work elsewhere.

 

This is the output generated by the Submit button in two flavors:
