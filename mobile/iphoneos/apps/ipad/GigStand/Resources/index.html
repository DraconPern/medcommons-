GigStand 1.0 Release Notes

for musicians, by musicians

Why GigStand?

GigStand is a digital musicstand for musicians and other performers. It lets you carry every bit of paper you've been schlepping to practices, sessions, and gigs in your iPhone or even better, in your iPad. It lets you and your bandmates share and exchange content and setlists in a controlled manner. GigStand works even when the Internet isn't available.

Early Days

I went to a wedding a while back. The band was great. The bandleader had a dozen boxes of index cards with multiple parts scored out for thousands of tunes. These were not old guys, yet they were fiddling with paper like it was 1955

A friend who plays in a jam band had to go to India for several months on business and I was asked to fill in for the mostly weekly Grateful Dead/Bob Dylan jams they've been holding for years. When I walked into this setting I saw they all had extra large white 3 ring binders obscenely stuffed full of paper of all sorts. I couldn't imagine making a copy of this beast.

Frankly, after a long career spent mostly in IT, I'd given up on the notion of purging paper from my life or anyone else's. Maybe my grandkids won't have much use for it. But why not try to get rid of all this paper at least when it comes to Dylan and Co?

This wasn't the prime motivator to actually do something, but it pushed me over the top.

I've been playing in PlanB, a Classic Rock Cover Band for six or seven years now. Some of the guys have perfect memory and / or perfect pitch. I have neither.

Without a book of crib sheets of various sorts I'd have been out on my ass. Our first go was always to some well known, ad-ridden websites which had the Top 500 tunes we needed. After a while it became necessary to work on these tunes a bit, and get them printed and viewable in a decent form and without the very distracting ads.

So I collected a lot of paper, and a bunch of us shared our tune info via printed excel spreadsheets, and then finally, when the iPhone appeared, we made a website with single page renditions of all our tunes.

This almost worked, except it seemed wherever we played the internet was very unreliable, and there was a lot of embarrassment without power adapters and still there was a need for paper backup.

And frankly, the iPhone was a bit small for anyone over the age of 40 except in the case of emergencies. By the time the iPad was announced, we were desperate for something we could actually use and already had a strong set of requirements for GigStand

  • Run Perfectly Even When Offline
  • Make It Easy For BandMates to Share Tunes and Setlists
  • Easy to See While Performing
  • Easy to Control Even While Performing

Our early efforts met none of these requirements. They were a hassle to use and somewhat dangerous. This is our second, or third effort.

Content Input Methods

Next

From Builtin Samples

  1. Go To Actions > Builtin Samples
  2. Select and Confirm One or More Samples
  3. Samples are Now in iTunes Inbox
  4. process iTunes Inbox when ready

iTunes Sharing

Prepare all your content on your desktop or laptop system before connecting your mobile device

On Your PC or Mac

  1. start iTunes if necessary
  2. plug your mobile device into iTunes
  3. select your mobile device when it appears and go to Apps
  4. scroll down to the iTunes Sharing Window, select GigStand from the list of Apps and double click
  5. drag files from your PC or MAC into the right hand iTunes Sharing Window

On Your Mobile Device

  1. from Actions, inspect and edit the iTunes Inbox
  2. from Actions, push the "process iTunes Inbox" button
  3. take a break, this could take minutes
  4. note incorporation of new content

Supported Input File Types

Next

STL File Type

STL files are produced by GigStand as an email attachment to any SetList that is mailed by the user

Any STL files in the iTunes Inbox are processed by GigStand and incorporated as additional GigStand SetLists

Separate the STL attachment from the email body on your desktop or laptop and drag just the attachment into iTunes Sharing as detailed above

ZIP File Type

Prepare all your content on your desktop or laptop system before connecting your mobile device. Each incoming ZIP file maps directly to one GigStand Archive

Special Files in the ZIP

These files are optional, but if present, make the ZIP file more useful for the end user

Logo File

If a file named --logo--.jpeg, --logo--.jpg, --logo--.png, or --logo--.gif is present in the zip file, it is used as the logo for the archive in GigStand.

Header Markup File

If a file named --header--.html is present, it is incorporated into the html headers of all html files in the zip file

Info File

If a file named --info--.html is present, it is display when information about the archive is requested. It will typically contain attribution back to the original content source

Filename Processing

Individual files are handled differently based on the ZIP file name, and potentially, the name of the tune itself.
